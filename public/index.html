The Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)

HIV is a virus that attacks the immune system, which is the body’s natural defense system. This means HIV interferes with your body’s ability to fight infection and disease.

When left untreated,

HIV leads
to AIDS

AIDS stands for Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome. Having HIV does not mean you have AIDS but when HIV is left untreated and completely breaks down your immune system over several years, it leads to AIDS.

Stage One -
Primary Infection

Acute HIV

This stage is around one to four weeks after getting HIV. These flu-like symptoms can be so mild that you don’t notice them but the amount of virus in your bloostream is quite high. As a result, the infection spreads more easily.
Fever
Body rash
Sore throat
Swollen lymph glands
Headache
Diarrhea
Joint aches and pains
Muscle pain
Coughs
Night sweats
Many people do not have any symptoms or infections during this time, even if HIV is still present in the body. This stage can last for many years if you’re not receiving antiretroviral therapy (ART).

Stage Three -

Symptomatic HIV

By this stage, a person’s immune system is severely damaged. They will. Be more likely to get serious infections or diseases that the body would otherwise be able to fight off.
Fever
Fatigue
Swollen lymph glands
Diarrhea
Weight loss
Oral yeast infection
Shingles
Pneumonia
Night sweats
Mouth and skin problems
